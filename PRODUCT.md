# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Saudi urban residents, primarily 20–40, using the app on mobile in casual moments: deciding where to go tonight, planning a weekend trip, coordinating a group outing. They are bilingual (Arabic/English), culturally local, and increasingly expect premium digital experiences on par with global travel apps. They use the app in ambient conditions — sunlit homes, cafes, moving cars — so the interface must work under both bright and dim light.

## Product Purpose

Tripo is an AI-enhanced local discovery and booking platform for the Saudi market. It surfaces micro-escapes (1–3 hour experiences) and longer journeys: places, tours, rentals, and events. Beyond booking, it handles group trip coordination, split expenses, communities, and AI-assisted planning. Success means a user opens the app and immediately sees something they want to do today.

## Brand Personality

Aspirational, Local, Refined.

The voice is warm but confident. Not promotional. Not tourist-brochure generic. It speaks like a well-traveled Saudi friend who knows the best spots and wants to share them.

## Anti-references

- Booking.com and Hotels.com: transactional, information-dense, no personality
- Generic Airbnb clone aesthetics: white cards, star ratings as the only visual accent, price-first hierarchy
- Corporate travel dashboards: tables, sidebar navigation, "business tool" energy
- Neon-on-black nightlife app aesthetic: Tripo is premium travel, not nightclub promotion
- Travel apps that treat Saudi Arabia as a generic Middle East backdrop rather than specific, named places

## Design Principles

1. **Place before price.** The destination, the feeling, the name — these appear first. Price and logistics follow. Every card leads with visual evidence, not commercial information.
2. **Local specificity over global polish.** Generic stock-photo aesthetics fail here. Named places (Hegra, Edge of the World, Al-Balad) with real images signal local knowledge. Vague "luxury travel" imagery signals the opposite.
3. **Dark is earned, not default.** The dark theme exists because Tripo users plan escapes in the evening, under warm indoor light, building anticipation. The navy depth amplifies that mood. It is not dark because travel apps look cool dark.
4. **Bilingual without compromise.** Arabic RTL and English LTR are equally designed. No component is designed in one direction and adapted in the other. Both are primary.
5. **Community alongside discovery.** Tripo is not just a booking engine. Group coordination, communities, and social proof are first-class features, not tabs buried in a More sheet.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum (existing focus styles and aria-labels are in place)
- RTL and LTR layouts fully supported at the component level
- Touch targets ≥44px on all interactive elements
- Reduced-motion support where animations are non-trivial
